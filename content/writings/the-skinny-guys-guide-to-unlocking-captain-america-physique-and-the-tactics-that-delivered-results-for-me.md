<h2 class="wp-block-heading">[1] Eat until you puke</h2>

<p><br>Amidst the noise and confusion surrounding <em>nutrition</em>, let me share a simple truth: it's all about the math.<br><br>The science behind nourishment boils down to a <strong>straightforward equation</strong> that even a kindergartener could grasp.</p>

<blockquote class="wp-block-quote">
<p>Calorie in &gt; Calorie out: You gain weight (enter the caloric surplus)<br>Calorie out &gt; Calorie in: You lose weight (enter the caloric deficit)</p>
</blockquote>

<p class="has-tertiary-background-color has-background">üí° Calories are units of energy found in the food we eat. When we eat, our body breaks down food into smaller components, which are then converted into energy which in turn fuels our bodily functions.</p>

<p><br>Forget the myriad of complexities people often associate with weight control.<br><br>Focus on this fundamental principle of being in a <strong>moderate caloric surplus of <a href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/bulking#preliminary-steps:~:text=For%20example%2C%20if%20you%20need%203%2C000%20daily%20calories%20per%20day%20to%20maintain%20weight%2C%20you%20should%20aim%20to%20consume%203%2C300%E2%80%933%2C600%20instead%2C%20depending%20on%20your%20experience%20level." target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">300 kcal</a></strong> and supplement it with these essential factors to enhance your understanding:</p>

<ul>
<li>Not all calories are created equal. Prioritize <a href="https://www.heart.org/en/healthy-living/healthy-eating/eat-smart/nutrition-basics/how-can-i-eat-more-nutrient-dense-foods#:~:text=That's%20where%20the%20saying%20%E2%80%9Cempty,fat%2C%20added%20sugars%20and%20sodium.)" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">nutrient-dense calories</a> over empty ones.</li>

<li>Focus on quality as much as quantity by choosing <a href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/junk-food-vs-healthy-food#what-are-healthy-foods" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">whole foods</a> over processed ones.</li>

<li>Steer clear of <a href="https://www.nhs.uk/live-well/eat-well/how-to-eat-a-balanced-diet/eat-less-saturated-fat/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">saturated fats</a>, <a href="https://www.mayoclinic.org/diseases-conditions/high-blood-cholesterol/in-depth/trans-fat/art-20046114" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">trans fats</a>, and <a href="https://www.medicalnewstoday.com/articles/325171" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">simple carbs</a>, but not forgetting ‚Äú<a href="https://betterhumans.pub/kale-wont-kill-you-sugar-isn-t-poison-ignore-silly-food-claims-about-harm-4c725c65dd9c#:~:text=volume%20of%20consumption.-,The%20Dosage%20Makes%20the%20Poison,-The%20next%20time" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">portion is poison</a>‚Äù.</li>

<li>Construct your meals around a foundation of <a href="https://www.healthline.com/nutrition/how-much-protein-per-day" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">protein</a>.</li>

<li>Stay adequately hydrated by <a href="https://www.healthline.com/health/food-nutrition/why-is-water-important" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">drinking enough water</a> throughout the day.</li>
</ul>

<h2 class="wp-block-heading">[2] Stop running, immediately</h2>

<p><br>In my quest for a <em>healthier</em> and more <em>muscular</em> physique, I mistakenly opted for <strong>running</strong> as my primary form of exercise.<br><br>Little did I know that I was <em>undermining</em> my own progress by neglecting the power of resistance training.</p>

<p>Consider the difference between <strong>sprinters</strong> and <strong>long-distance runners</strong>. The former, with their explosive power and well-defined muscles, embody the results I was seeking.<br><br>It became clear that my preference for galloping 5 kilometers every day across <a href="https://greatruns.com/new-delhi-bonta-park/" target="_blank" rel="noreferrer noopener">my favourite park</a> back in Delhi Campus, accompanied by minimal bodyweight exercises, was <strong>NOT aligning</strong> with my goals.<br><br>Why was resistance training so important for me, you ask?<br><br>Unlike <strong>running</strong>, which primarily improves <em>cardiovascular endurance</em>, <strong>resistance training</strong> stimulates <em>muscle growth</em> and <em>development</em>. It enables progressive overload, where the muscles are consistently challenged with increased resistance, leading to <em>hypertrophy</em> and <em>strength gains</em>.<br><br>Even when I started prioritizing <em>resistance training</em>, I stuck with <strong>bodyweight exercises</strong> and couldn‚Äôt have been more wrong.<br><br>While exercises like push-ups, pull-ups, and squats are excellent compound movements, they have limitations in terms of <strong>progressive overload</strong> and targeting <strong>specific muscle groups</strong>.<br><br>There's only so much you can do in terms of repetitions within a set. To build muscle effectively, <strong>progressive overloading</strong> is crucial.<br><br>And it requires providing stimulation that your muscles haven't grown accustomed to.<br><br>Despite being proficient in push-ups, pull-ups, and squats due to my background as a skater, gymnast, and badminton player, I could perform <em>50 push-ups</em> in a single go, yet still not achieve significant muscular hypertrophy.<br><br>It was only when I finally made the decision to join a gym (last month) that I witnessed astounding results.</p>
