###### Embrace the Suck

<br>When it comes to building mental toughness, the first rule of thumb is to "embrace the suck."<br><br>Life's not always a bed of roses, and challenges have a knack for showing up unannounced.<br><br>But instead of running for cover, face them head-on like a warrior.

<figure><blockquote>
Shift your mindset from seeing challenges as stumbling blocks to viewing them as stepping stones for growth.
</blockquote></figure>

The harder the challenge, the more badass you'll become.<br><br>You will start witnessing the changes in your confidence from day one when you start taking on the discomfort of picking a new project at work or facing your fears.<br><br>These situations can trigger the release of adrenaline and cortisol, which are commonly known as stress hormones.<br><br>They help us to be alert, focused, and prepared to take action when faced with challenges.<br><br>It's like having your own built-in cheerleaders, urging you to "embrace the suck" and push through the discomfort. <br><br>Your body is in on the game too, with all its functions geared up to support you in your quest for mental toughness. So, what's stopping you now?

<br>Forge a code of discipline that you live by, and watch your mental toughness soar to new heights.<br><br>Set clear standards for yourself and make them non-negotiable.<br><br>Act as if somebody is watching a documentary of your life.<br><br>Whether it's smashing your fitness goals, slaying it at work, or conquering your relationships, refuse to settle for anything less than excellence.

<blockquote>

Motivation is temporary, discipline is permanent.

</blockquote>

But how do you stick to your code, even when you feel like hitting the snooze button or throwing in the towel?<br><br>You create a killer daily routine that's packed with habits that will level up your mental game.<br><br>Small habits make up your routine, routine keeps you disciplined and discipline takes your places.
